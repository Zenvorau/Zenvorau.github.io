Simple Weekly Content Automation

A low-risk system for researching ideas, drafting posts, reviewing claims, and publishing useful content.

This is a workflow, not a money guarantee. The goal is to reduce repeated manual work while keeping human review for anything factual, legal, financial, or promotional.

The Weekly Loop

1. Collect Ideas

Keep a running list of article ideas in a simple document, Notion page, spreadsheet, or GitHub file.

Example idea format: topic / audience / why it matters / source links

2. Pick One Useful Topic

Choose one topic that is practical, low-risk, and easy to explain. Avoid topics that require expert legal, medical, or financial advice.

3. Gather Sources

Before drafting, collect official pages or primary sources. For tool pricing and product limits, use official pricing or documentation pages whenever possible.

4. Draft the Article

Use AI to create a first draft, but treat it as a draft only. The draft should be checked for spelling, broken links, outdated details, and unsupported claims.

5. Review Risky Claims

Slow down on claims about price, laws, health, finance, safety, platform rules, or guarantees. Either verify them or remove them.

6. Publish

Add the article as an HTML file in the posts folder, then link to it from the homepage.

7. Send or Save a Newsletter Note

If the article is useful, turn it into a short newsletter. If the newsletter is not ready yet, save the summary for later.

A Simple Schedule

  • Monday: collect ideas
  • Tuesday: choose one topic and gather sources
  • Wednesday: draft
  • Thursday: review and edit
  • Friday: publish and update homepage links

What Can Be Automated Later

  • Creating draft files from templates
  • Checking links
  • Listing posts automatically on the homepage
  • Creating a weekly newsletter draft
  • Running a scheduled GitHub Action to prepare draft ideas

What Should Stay Manual

  • Approving final factual claims
  • Approving affiliate links or money-related wording
  • Checking that sources are current
  • Making sure the article is useful, not spam

The Main Rule

Automate the repetitive parts. Review the parts where mistakes would matter.
